# [Product Name] — 3-minute pitch outline

> Fillable template. Copy into your slide tool of choice. Each slide's on-slide text goes on the slide; the speaker script is what you say out loud; the transition is the sentence that glues this slide to the next one.
>
> Target runtime: **2:30–2:45**. Don't use all 3:00 — give yourself breathing room.

---

## Slide 1 — Intro / Hook  `[0:00 – 0:15]`

**On-slide:** Logo · one-liner · *(optional)* founder photo + name

**Speaker script:**
> Hi, I'm [name], founder of [product]. We're [one-line what-we-do].

**Transition:** *"So let me tell you what we're seeing."*

---

## Slide 2 — Problem  `[0:15 – 0:45]`

**On-slide:** One headline problem · consequence · data point · evocative image

**Speaker script:**
> [Name a specific user situation]. [Stat proving the pain]. [Why it matters — lost money / lost time / missed opportunity].

**Transition:** *"And that's exactly what we're solving."*

---

## Slide 3 — Value Proposition  `[0:45 – 0:55]`

**On-slide:** One sentence, benefit-facing, large type

**Speaker script:**
> [Product] lets you [do the user benefit] — [the wedge that makes it different].

**Transition:** *"Here's how it works."*

---

## Slide 4 — Features / How it works  `[0:55 – 1:15]`

**On-slide:** 3 features-as-benefits (or 3-step user journey) · reveal with animation

**Speaker script:**
> First, [feature 1]. Then, [feature 2]. And finally, [feature 3].

**Transition:** *"Let me show you."*

---

## Slide 5 — Demo  `[1:15 – 1:45]`

**On-slide:** Screen recording or phone mockup · caption with in-situ use case · **no background music**

**Speaker script:** (narrate the live flow, calmly)
> [Step 1 happening on screen]. [Step 2]. [Step 3]. That's it.

**Transition:** *"And this isn't a small market."*

---

## Slide 6 — Market  `[1:45 – 1:55]`

**On-slide:** User categories with numbers · intersection or venn · NOT TAM/SAM/SOM

**Speaker script:**
> We're targeting [user category 1] and [user category 2] — that's [number] potential users in the intersection.

**Transition:** *"So how do we make money?"*

---

## Slide 7 — Business Model  `[1:55 – 2:05]`

**On-slide:** One-line mechanism (e.g., "2% transaction fee on every payment")

**Speaker script:**
> [One-line mechanism]. [Optional: one-line unit economic, e.g., "at scale, that's $X ARR per 10k users"].

**Transition:** *"And we're already doing it."*

---

## Slide 8 — Traction  `[2:05 – 2:25]`

**On-slide:** 3 real metrics with growth arrow · NO X/Twitter followers

**Speaker script:**
> We have [revenue / real user number]. Growing [growth rate] week over week. [Bonus: comparison to industry average, e.g., "10× the retention of comparable apps"].

**Transition:** *"Here's how we scale this."*

---

## Slide 9 — Growth Strategy  `[2:25 – 2:35]`

**On-slide:** Target + 3 concrete channels

**Speaker script:**
> Our target is [milestone]. We get there through [channel 1], [channel 2], [channel 3].

**Transition:** *"And here's what ships next."*

---

## Slide 10 — Roadmap  `[2:35 – 2:45]`

**On-slide:** Dated milestones (Q2, Q3, Q4) · one big thing per quarter
*(This is the only slide where future tense is allowed.)*

**Speaker script:**
> Next quarter, [big thing 1]. In Q3, [big thing 2]. By Q4, [big thing 3].

**Transition:** *"We're the team to do it."*

---

## Slide 11 — Team  `[2:45 – 2:55]`

**On-slide:** **One outstanding achievement** as the headline · team faces/roles secondary

**Speaker script:**
> We're the team that [outstanding achievement]. [One line of support — e.g., "we did it once, we're doing it again"].

**Transition:** *"One more thing before we go."*

---

## Slide 12 — Conclusion + CTA  `[2:55 – 3:00]`

**On-slide:** Product one-liner echo · QR code to Legends.fund / product page / longer demo · specific next action

**Speaker script:**
> [Product] — [one memorable line]. Scan to [CTA].

---

## Optional slides (insert only if they add signal)

### Trends
**On-slide:** 1–3 growth stats showing the market is accelerating
**Position:** after Market
**Time cost:** ~5 seconds

### Competition
**On-slide:** 2–3 competitors in a compare-on-one-axis matrix
**Position:** after Market (or after Trends)
**Time cost:** ~10 seconds
**Skip if:** no obvious competitor the judge would ask about

### Partners / Awards
**On-slide:** logos **with one-line context** on what each does for you
**Position:** after Traction
**Time cost:** ~5–10 seconds
**Skip if:** partners are thin or it's just co-marketing

### Authority-first opening (for exceptional traction)
If your traction is outstanding ($1M+ revenue, 100k+ users, named backer), move Slide 8 (Traction) to Slide 2, *before* the Problem. Establish authority, *then* pitch.

---

## Pre-submission checklist

- [ ] Runtime timed at ≤3:00 (target 2:30–2:45)
- [ ] Hook in first 15 seconds
- [ ] No "trying to", "hope to", "aim to", "would", "should"
- [ ] Future tense appears only on Slide 10 (Roadmap)
- [ ] Demo before 2:00, 20–30 sec, no music
- [ ] Traction uses real metrics, no X followers
- [ ] Business model slide present
- [ ] Team slide leads with an achievement, not bios
- [ ] CTA at the end, not "thank you"
- [ ] One memorable line embedded
- [ ] Rehearsed 50× (no joke) — record, wait 24h, listen, watch, fix, repeat
- [ ] $30 mic, clean background, full-screen deck
- [ ] Legends.fund / product-page QR on closing slide (if Colosseum Frontier)
